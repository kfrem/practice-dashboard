MTD for Income Tax Self Assessment — Phase 1 now live

Self-employed individuals and landlords with income over £50,000 must now use MTD-compatible software and submit quarterly updates. Paper self assessment has ceased for this group.

Read HMRC guidance →

Companies HouseAML

Identity verification mandatory for new incorporations

Directors, PSCs, and those filing on behalf of companies must now verify their identity with Companies House for new incorporations. Existing officers have a transition period running through late 2026.

Read Companies House guidance →
Apr26
PayrollEmployment

National Living Wage rises to £12.21/hour

The NLW increases to £12.21 per hour for workers aged 21 and over from 6 April 2026. Employer National Insurance threshold changes also take effect this month — all payroll software must be updated before the first April payroll run.

View current rates →
Apr27
MTDPlan now

MTD for Income Tax — Phase 2 extends to £30,000+ (April 2027)

Practices should now identify all self-employed clients and landlords with income between £30,000 and £50,000. This group must be enrolled by April 2027. Beginning the conversation now avoids the pressure that affected Phase 1 preparation.

HMRC MTD timeline →

HMRC AML supervision — intensifying across the accountancy sector

Practices must maintain a written firm-wide risk assessment, individual client risk ratings, documented CDD and EDD records, PEP and sanctions screening evidence, and records of ongoing monitoring. HMRC's supervision visits have increased significantly in frequency.

Making Tax Digital — Full Timeline

Where we are and what your practice needs to plan for next

✓
April 2019
MTD for VAT — businesses above the VAT threshold
Mandatory for VAT-registered businesses with taxable turnover above £85,000. Digital records and MTD-compatible software required.
Complete
✓
April 2022
MTD for VAT — all VAT-registered businesses
Extended to all VAT-registered businesses regardless of turnover, including those voluntarily registered below the threshold.
Complete — mandatory for all
▶
April 2026 — Now live
MTD for Income Tax — Phase 1 (income over £50,000)
Self-employed individuals and landlords with gross income over £50,000 must now maintain digital records and submit quarterly updates to HMRC using MTD-compatible software. Paper self assessment has ceased for this group.
Live — clients must be enrolled now
!
April 2027 — Plan now
MTD for Income Tax — Phase 2 (income over £30,000)
Extends to self-employed individuals and landlords with income over £30,000. This group is significantly larger than Phase 1. Practices should identify all affected clients now and begin migration planning. Leaving this until 2027 will create significant workload pressure.
12 months — identify clients now
○
April 2028 — Confirmed in principle
MTD for Income Tax — Phase 3 (income over £20,000)
Further extension to individuals with income over £20,000. Date confirmed in principle by HMRC subject to review. General partnerships also expected to be mandated in a future phase — no date confirmed.
Monitor HMRC announcements
○
Date not yet confirmed
MTD for Corporation Tax
MTD for Corporation Tax is under active development by HMRC. A pilot programme has run and is under review. No mandatory date has been confirmed. Practices should monitor HMRC's Making Tax Digital for Corporation Tax consultation pages.
No mandatory date set
